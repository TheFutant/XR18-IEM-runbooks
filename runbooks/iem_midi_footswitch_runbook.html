MIDI Footswitch Runbook

M-Vave Chocolate → LEKATO WIDI → XR18. One-time setup, then a simple mute test, then scene recall once you have scenes to recall.

▸Phase 1 · One-Time Setup
Easy to miss: the XR18's MIDI DIN input does nothing to the mixer until you flip this on.
Enable "DIN Rx" in Mixing Station's MIDI config
Without this, MIDI at the DIN port passes straight through to USB and never touches the mixer — the whole chain would look broken when it's really just this checkbox.
Plug WIDI's MIDI A into the XR18
5-pin DIN cable from MIDI A into the XR18's MIDI IN jack.
Keep MIDI B unplugged for now
MIDI B (the USB/computer half) shouldn't be powered on while pairing MIDI A to the Chocolate — it can grab the connection first.
▸Phase 2 · Pair Chocolate to WIDI
The Chocolate can only hold one Bluetooth MIDI connection at a time. If it's already linked to the iPad from a CubeSuite session, it won't be free to pair here.
Disconnect Chocolate from the iPad
iPad Bluetooth settings → disconnect or forget the Chocolate/FootCtrl. Close CubeSuite too, just in case it's holding the link open.
Power on MIDI A
Blue LED blinking = ready and waiting to pair.
Power on the Chocolate
Give it 10-15 seconds to auto-pair with MIDI A — no button combo needed.
Confirm solid LED on MIDI A
Blinking → solid usually signals a locked connection.
▸Phase 3 · Main Mute Test
No scenes needed for this one — it's the fastest way to confirm the whole chain works end to end.
CUBESUITE
Program a footswitch: Main Mute
Channel 2 CC 31 Value 127 = mute on Value 0 = mute off
If the Chocolate's toggle behavior isn't reliable for one switch, use two footswitches — one fixed at 127, one fixed at 0.
Test standing next to the mixer
Tap the footswitch, watch the main fader/mute indicator in Mixing Station.
Test at real stage distance
Walk to where you'd actually stand and confirm the Bluetooth link holds before trusting it live.
Chain confirmed end to end
Chocolate → Bluetooth → WIDI → MIDI DIN → XR18, all verified working.
▸Phase 4 · Your First Two Scenes
Start simple — two scenes, not twenty-two. Expand into per-song FX profiles later once this split feels natural to use.
SCENE 1 · PC 1
Practice
Looser monitor balance built for hearing mistakes and locking in parts — not a polished show mix. Whatever you actually want dialed in for rehearsal.
SCENE 2 · PC 2
Gig
Your real show mix — full main output active, whatever FX profile suits most of the setlist as the default.
Save both scenes in Mixing Station
Confirm which PC number each one lands on before wiring up footswitches in the next phase.
Once Gig vs. Practice feels solid, the natural next step is splitting "Gig" further into the per-song FX profiles from earlier — Dry/Rock, Ballad, Anthemic — using scoped snapshots so they only touch FX sends, not your gain staging.
▸Phase 5 · Assign to Footswitches
Once Gig and Practice both exist as saved scenes, wire them up to the Chocolate.
CUBESUITE
Program a footswitch: Scene Recall
Channel 1 Program Change PC number = scene number (1–64)
E.g. footswitch A → PC 2 → recalls whatever's saved as Scene 2 in Mixing Station.
Confirm scene numbers in Mixing Station
Practice should sit at PC 1, Gig at PC 2 — double check before assigning footswitches, in case the save order shifted anything.
Test each footswitch against its scene
Confirm the right scene loads for each press — a channel or PC mismatch is the most common miss here.
▸Quick Reference
"Nothing's happening when I press the footswitch"
Check DIN Rx is still enabled — it can get toggled off if the XR18 config resets.
"Chocolate won't pair to WIDI"
Something else Bluetooth-MIDI-capable is probably powered on nearby and grabbing the connection first — check the iPad and MIDI B are both off.
"Need to reprogram the Chocolate"
Disconnect it from WIDI first, pair to the iPad, open CubeSuite, make changes, then disconnect from iPad and re-pair to WIDI.
Vocabulary refresher
PC = Program Change, recalls a preset/scene. CC = Continuous Controller, used for faders, mutes, and on/off toggles.
